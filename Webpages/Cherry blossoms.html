Commonly known as Hanami(はなみ/花見)in Japanese, cherry blossom festivals are significant traditions that are held all over Japan every spring. From mid-March to mid-April, thousands of people flock to parks and gardens to enjoy the fleeting beauty of Sakura (さくら/桜). Friends and families huddle down under blooming cherry trees with homemade food and drinks. Hanami Dango (花見団子),Sakura Mochi (桜餅) and Matcha (抹茶) are the most popular foods during flower-viewing picnics.

Since cherry trees only bloom in early spring and its bloom period often last no more than two weeks, they are always regarded as a symbol of transience and nobleness. Although cherry is not the national flower of Japan, it is widely adored by Japanese general public. Today, Hanami acts as an annual celebration to appreciate the temporal beauty of nature and to welcome the arrival of spring.
